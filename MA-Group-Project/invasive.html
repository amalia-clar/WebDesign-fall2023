Invasive Species

What are Invasive Species?

Invasive species are animals that do not originate or belong in a habitat or area being
not native but have gotten there anyways and are causing harm to their new environment.

How do they harm the environment?

Invasive species are throwing an ecosystem out of balance and causing mass destruction
which can cause the extinction of native animals and lower natural resources. Besides
ruining the environment, they also cause impacts on humans, like huge negative economic
impacts and disruptions of human frequented places like beaches or trails.

What can you do?

On an individual level you could not transport different wild animals, plants, and other
organisms around, whether it be through airplanes, cars, or just walking. You should also
not release animals like pets which are not native to the environment as they can cause huge
damage and start a population there. You could also donate to organizations dedicated to this cause.

What can the community do?

On a group level there could be more efforts to not only stop the progress of Invasive
species but to also get them. A community could make organized efforts to cut down invasive plants
or with proper precautions help with capturing and/or moving animals back to their proper habitat.
